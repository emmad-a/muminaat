## 1. Ijarah (Hire & Leasing) — 15 Questions

### Q: What is ijarah and what are its pillars? (ijarah-definition)
- ✅ Accurate — Hanafi one-pillar vs. majority four-pillar distinction is correct. Quran 28:27 and 65:6 references are real. Hadith of hiring the guide during Hijrah (Abdullah ibn Urayqit) is in Bukhari. Scholar attributions are correct.

### Q: What are the conditions for a valid ijarah contract? (ijarah-conditions-validity)
- ✅ Accurate — Conditions are correctly stated for each madhab. Maliki reliance on custom ('urf) is well-documented. Shafi'i strictness on specification is correct per Mughni al-Muhtaj.

### Q: Difference between ijarah on property and persons (ijarah-property-vs-persons)
- ✅ Accurate — Ajir khass/mushtarak distinction is correct for Hanafi and Hanbali. The Shafi'i classification (waridah 'ala al-'ayn vs. 'ala al-dhimmah) is accurate and genuinely distinctive.

### Q: Must rent be paid in advance? (ijarah-advance-rent)
- ✅ Accurate — Maliki requirement of advance payment for ijarah 'ala al-dhimmah (to avoid bay' al-kali' bil-kali') is correctly stated. Hanafi/Hanbali incremental default is accurate. Shafi'i default of immediate due date is correct per Nawawi.

### Q: Rights and obligations of lessor and lessee (ijarah-rights-obligations)
- ✅ Accurate — Maintenance allocation is correctly described. Hanbali permissiveness on subletting is accurate per al-Mughni.

### Q: When does ijarah terminate? (ijarah-termination)
- ✅ Accurate — The Hanafi uniqueness of allowing cancellation for excuse ('udhr) and termination by death is a well-known distinctive position. The majority view of ijarah surviving death is correct. This is one of the sharpest Hanafi vs. majority differences.

### Q: Ijarah with unspecified duration (ijarah-unknown-duration)
- ⚠️ Needs verification — The Shafi'i rulingType is set to "haram" for an open-ended ijarah. While the Shafi'i school does invalidate contracts with unknown duration (due to gharar), the term "haram" is technically about the moral ruling, whereas the contract issue is one of validity (batil/fasid). The rulingType should arguably be "wajib" (it is wajib to specify the duration) rather than "haram." This is a semantic issue rather than a substantive error.

### Q: Can a lessee sublease? (ijarah-subleasing)
- ✅ Accurate — Hanafi permissiveness is correct. Maliki default permission with contractual override is correct. The note about the Shafi'i mu'tamad permitting subleasing is accurate (per al-Ramli's Nihayat al-Muhtaj).

### Q: Defect discovered in leased asset (ijarah-defect-discovered)
- ✅ Accurate — Shafi'i requirement of immediate exercise (fawriyyah) of the defect option is correctly distinguished from other schools. All positions are well-sourced.

### Q: Lessee liability for damage (ijarah-lessee-liability)
- ✅ Accurate — The consensus on the lessee as amin (trustee) is correct. All four positions are consistent with the classical texts.

### Q: Ijara sukuk (ijarah-sukuk)
- ✅ Accurate — AAOIFI Shariah Standard No. 17 is the correct reference for sukuk. The emphasis on genuine asset-backing and real risk transfer is the standard contemporary position across all fatwa bodies.

### Q: Car leasing and rent-to-own (ijarah-car-leasing)
- ✅ Accurate — AAOIFI Shariah Standard No. 9 is correct for ijarah muntahiyah bil-tamlik. OIC Fiqh Academy Resolution No. 110 is a real resolution. The requirement for separate contracts (lease + promise to transfer) is the standard AAOIFI position.

### Q: Renting for haram purpose (ijarah-renting-for-haram)
- ✅ Accurate — The Hanafi internal distinction between Abu Hanifa (technically valid but makruh tahrimi) and Sahibayn (invalid) is correct. Maliki sadd al-dhara'i application is accurate. This is a genuine multi-madhab divergence.

### Q: Inflation and rent increase (ijarah-inflation-rent-increase)
- ✅ Accurate — No unilateral mid-term increase is the consensus. Pre-agreed escalation clauses are the standard contemporary fatwa position.

### Q: Hiring non-Muslims (ijarah-hiring-non-muslim)
- ✅ Accurate — The Prophet hiring Abdullah ibn Urayqit (a mushrik guide) is in Sahih al-Bukhari. Complete consensus is correctly stated.

## 2. Wakalah (Agency & Proxy) — 7 Questions

### Q: What is wakalah and its pillars? (wakalah-definition)
- ✅ Accurate — Quran 18:19 (Companions of the Cave) is real. The delegation of Abu Rafi' (Maliki evidence) and Urwa al-Bariqi (Shafi'i evidence) are authentic narrations. One pillar (Hanafi) vs. four (majority) is correct.

### Q: What can be delegated? (wakalah-what-can-be-delegated)
- ⚠️ Needs verification — The Hanafi position states a husband can delegate the right to divorce to the wife (tafwid al-talaq). This is technically correct and is a well-known Hanafi position, but it is more precisely called "tafwid" (delegation of divorce) rather than wakalah in some texts. The distinction between tafwid and wakalah in divorce is nuanced. The statement is substantially correct but could be more precise.

### Q: Wakalah with/without compensation (wakalah-with-without-compensation)
- ✅ Accurate — The distinction between gratuitous (ja'iz/non-binding) and compensated (lazim/binding) wakalah is correct across all schools.

### Q: Agent liability (wakalah-agent-liability)
- ✅ Accurate — Agent as amin (trustee) is consensus. The Maliki evidentiary principle (agent's word accepted with oath) is correctly stated.

### Q: Termination of wakalah (wakalah-termination)
- ✅ Accurate — All causes of termination are correct. The notification requirement (acts before notification are valid) is consistent across schools and correctly stated.

### Q: Wakalah in Islamic finance (wakalah-islamic-finance)
- ⚠️ Needs verification — AAOIFI Shariah Standard No. 23 is referenced for wakalah bil-istithmar. However, AAOIFI Standard No. 23 is actually for "Agency and the Act of an Agent" (al-Wakalah). The content is correct but the standard number should be verified — some sources list it as Standard No. 23 and others as a different number depending on the AAOIFI edition. The substance is accurate.

### Q: Power of attorney (wakalah-power-of-attorney)
- ✅ Accurate — The spectrum from Hanbali (broadest interpretation) to Shafi'i (most restrictive) is correctly stated.

## 7. Kafalah & Hawalah (Guaranty & Debt Transfer) — 5 Questions

### Q: Kafalah definition and types (kafalah-definition)
- ✅ Accurate — Quran 12:72 is correctly cited. The hadith "al-za'im gharim" is in Sunan Abu Dawud. The difference between Shafi'i (subsidiary guarantee — demand debtor first) and Hanbali (creditor can pursue either immediately) is a genuine and important divergence.
- ⚠️ Needs verification — The claim that kafalah bil-nafs is "unique" to the Hanafi school is overstated. While the Hanafi school is most known for it, other schools also recognize personal surety (kafalah bil-nafs) to varying degrees. The Maliki and Hanbali schools also accept it. The uniqueness claim should be softened.

### Q: Compensation for guarantee (kafalah-compensation)
- ✅ Accurate — Classical consensus prohibiting fees for kafalah is correctly stated. The reasoning (resembles riba on lending one's creditworthiness) is the standard classical explanation. All four positions are consistent with the primary sources cited.

The Hanbali uniqueness (creditor is *obligated* to accept hawalah from a solvent payer) is genuine and correctly derived from the imperative form of the hadith. The Hanafi muqayyadah/mutlaqah distinction is correct.

### Q: Muqayyadah vs. mutlaqah (hawalah-muqayyadah-mutlaqah)
- ✅ Accurate — The Hanafi school's unique acceptance of hawalah mutlaqah as *true hawalah* (not merely kafalah) is correctly distinguished from the other schools. The practical consequences for the effect (discharge of transferor) are accurately noted.

### Q: Bank guarantees and letters of credit (kafalah-bank-guarantees)

## 8. Ila/Zihar/Li'an in Marriage — 3 Questions

### Q: Ila definition and consequences (marriage-ila-definition)
- ✅ Accurate — Quran 2:226-227 is correctly cited. The Hanafi uniqueness of *automatic* divorce after four months (without judicial action) vs. the majority position requiring deliberate act/judicial intervention is the central ila dispute and is correctly presented. This is one of the most well-known Hanafi distinctive positions in family law.

### Q: Zihar definition and expiation (marriage-zihar-definition)
- ✅ Accurate — Quran 58:1-4 (Surah al-Mujadilah) is correctly cited. The story of Khawlah bint Tha'labah and Aws ibn al-Samit is the canonical sabab al-nuzul. The three-tier kaffarah (free slave → fast two months → feed sixty poor) is correctly ordered. The Maliki extension to comparisons with any mahram (not just the mother) is a genuine distinctive position.

### Q: Li'an procedure and consequences (marriage-lian-definition)
- ✅ Accurate — Quran 24:6-9 is correctly cited. The five-oath procedure for each spouse is correctly described. The hadith of Hilal ibn Umayyah and Uwaimir al-Ajlani are the two canonical li'an incidents. The Shafi'i position that li'an is faskh (not talaq) is a genuine and important distinction correctly noted.
- ⚠️ Needs verification — The rulingType is "wajib" for all four madhabs. Li'an is a *procedure* initiated when a husband accuses his wife — "wajib" implies it is obligatory, but it is more accurately described as a procedure that *becomes* wajib if the husband makes the accusation and has no witnesses. The rulingType "wajib" is directionally correct but could be more precise. Some might argue "mubah" (since the husband has the choice to accuse or not) would be more appropriate for the initial step.
